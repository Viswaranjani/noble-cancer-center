Day Care Chemotherapy is a modern and patient-centered approach to cancer treatment, designed to deliver chemotherapy sessions in a outpatient setting. This treatment allows patients to receive the same high-quality care as inpatient chemotherapy but with added benefit of returning home the same day.

Unlike traditional inpatient chemotherapy, where hospitalization is required for extended periods, day care chemotherapy offers a convenient, cost-effective solution that minimizes disruption to daily life. With shorter treatment durations and tailored care, patients can focus on recovery while maintaining their routines and comfort.

Types of Chemeotherapy

Adjuvant Therapy - Adjuvant therapy is given after primary treatments like surgery or radiation. It aims to destroy remaining cancer cells that might lead to recurrence. This approach enhances the chances of long-term remission and recovery.

Curative Therapy - Curative therapy focuses on completely eradicating cancer and preventing its return. Often combined with surgery or radiation, it is used for early-stage cancers where remission is achievable. This method is tailored to aggressively target the disease for a full cure.

Neoadjuvant Therapy - Neoadjuvant therapy is administered before surgery or radiation to shrink tumors. This method makes surgical removal easier and more effective while reducing the invasiveness of the procedure. It also provides insight into how the tumor responds to treatment.

Palliative Therapy - Palliative therapy aims to improve the quality of life for patients with advanced-stage cancer. It reduces tumor size and alleviates symptoms like pain and discomfort. This method focuses on providing relief rather than achieving a cure.

Chemotherapy Infusions

Administered through a vein infusion (intravenously), using a needle inserted into a vein in the arm or chest.

Oral Chemotherapy

Pills and capsules containing chemotherapy drugs can be taken orally for some treatments.

Chemotherapy Injections

Medications are injected directly into the vein, similar to how a shot is administered.

Chemotherapy Creams

Creams or gels containing chemotherapy drugs are applied to treat certain skin cancers.

Localized Chemotherapy

Administered directly to a specific body part, such as the abdomen (intraperitoneal), chest cavity, or central nervous system.

Direct Tumor Administration

Chemotherapy drugs are applied directly to the cancer site or surgical site for targeted treatment.
