Comprehensive Cancer Care Begins Here

An oncology consultation is the first step in diagnosing and managing cancer with a personalized treatment approach. Our expert oncologists assess your medical history, review test results, and recommend the most effective treatment plan to ensure the best possible care.

Why is an Oncology Consultation Important?

Comprehensive Health Assessment - The oncologist will take a detailed medical history, asking about symptoms, family history, lifestyle factors, and any prior treatments.

Review of Diagnostic Tests - The doctor will analyze blood tests, biopsy results, imaging scans (CT, MRI, PET), and other reports to confirm the diagnosis and stage of cancer.

Treatment Discussion - Based on your specific cancer type and stage, the oncologist will explain the available treatment options, including chemotherapy, radiation therapy, targeted therapy, immunotherapy, and surgery.

Supportive Care Plan - Cancer treatment can have physical and emotional effects, so your oncologist will provide guidance on pain management, nutritional support, mental health counseling, and lifestyle modifications to improve your quality of life.

Follow-Up Plan - The doctor will schedule regular follow-ups to monitor treatment progress, manage side effects, and adjust the care plan if necessary.

Addressing Patient Concerns - The consultation is also a chance for you to ask any questions about your diagnosis, prognosis, or treatment.

Time Required for Oncology Consultation

  • Duration of Consultation: Typically, the consultation lasts between 30 minutes to 1 hour. The oncologist will review your medical history, discuss your diagnosis, and recommend treatment options.

  • Hospital Stay: In most cases, no overnight stay is required. Patients can return home after the consultation unless additional tests or procedures are needed.

  • Follow-Up Appointments: Follow-up visits are generally scheduled every 1 to 4 weeks, depending on your treatment plan and progress.
