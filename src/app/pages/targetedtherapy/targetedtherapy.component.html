Benefits of Targeted therapy
  • Targets specific cancer cells, sparing healthy cells.
  • Causes fewer side effects than traditional chemotherapy.
  • Works well for cancers with known genetic mutations.
Downsides of Targeted therapy
  • Cancer cells may develop resistance over time.
  • Not all cancers have identifiable genetic mutations or targets
  • Expensive and not all patients have access to the genetic testing

Patient Eligiblity and Selection Criteria for Targeted therapy

Genetic Mutations and Biomarkers - Patients whose tumors have specific genetic mutations or express certain proteins may be candidates for targeted therapy. Identifying these biomarkers involves tests to detect mutations like EGFR, HER2, or BRAF, which can be addressed with specific therapies.

Type and Stage of Cancer - The effectiveness of targeted therapy varies with cancer type and stage. Some treatments are approved for specific cancers, such as breast cancer, lung cancer, or melanoma, and may be more effective at certain stages.

Prior Treatment History - Patients who haven't responded to conventional treatments like chemotherapy or radiation may consider targeted therapies, especially if their cancer has actionable genetic alterations.

Availability of Targeted Agents -The presence of a specific targetable mutation must align with the availability of an approved targeted agent. In some cases, participation in clinical trials may be an option if no approved therapies exist for a particular target.

Overall Health Status -A patient's general health and organ function are crucial, as some targeted therapies can have side effects impacting organs like the liver or heart.

Signal Transduction Inhibitors

This disrupt the pathways that cancer cells use to grow and divide. For ex, imatinib treats chronic myeloid leukemia by targeting the BCR-ABL protein.

Gene Expression Modulators

These therapies alter the function of genes that are involved in cancer cell survival. Vorinostat, a histone deacetylase inhibitor, treats cutaneous T-cell lymphoma.

Apoptosis Inducers

It help trigger the natural process of cell death in cancer cells.For ex, Venetoclax treats chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL) by inhibiting the BCL-2 protein.

Angiogenesis Inhibitors

By blocking the formation of new blood vessels, angiogenesis inhibitors can starve tumors of the nutrients they need to grow. Bevacizumab (Avastin) is often used in the treatment of various cancers, including colorectal cancer

Monoclonal Antibodies

These are lab-made proteins that can bind to specific targets on cancer cells. Trastuzumab is a well-known monoclonal antibody used in treating HER2-positive breast cancer by specifically targeting the HER2 receptor
